## Interpretation

- **Corpus-wide winner: split_combined** (1.822) beats regular (1.785) on commentary-accuracy score — but the two pipelines cover different book sets (regular: 24 books; split: 20 books, zero overlap in coverage outside the 5 shared books), so this is largely a cross-corpus comparison.

- **split-a alone** is the single strongest split stage at 1.935 (nearly matching regular on overlap), while **split-b** drags averaged combined score down to 1.710.
- **Word count** differs sharply by pipeline: split_combined produces ~83-word BHTs (≈36% longer than regular's ≈61 words), driven by concatenating ~54 (split-a) + ~30 (split-b) tokens per verse.
- **quoteTokenProportion** is roughly flat across pipelines (~31.8%–32.6%), so quote-vs-paraphrase mix is not the differentiator.

**Headline:** Split-combined wins corpus-wide only because it covers different books; on the 5 books where we can compare head-to-head, the **regular pipeline scores higher** (1.94 vs 1.79) and produces BHTs ~25% shorter.
