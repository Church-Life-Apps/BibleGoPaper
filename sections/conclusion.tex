We have presented BibleGoAI, a multi-stage system for synthesizing concise, source-adherent summaries of biblical commentary using large language models, retrieval-augmented generation, and semantic similarity scoring. The system transforms a curated corpus of 14 commentators spanning four centuries into 30--80 word Brief Helpful Texts (BHTs) that are grounded in source material, quality-scored across multiple dimensions, and traceable via automated footnotes.

Our evaluation across 7{,}957 NT verses and the full 39-book OT corpus demonstrates that the pipeline produces consistent, high-quality summaries with strong commentary accuracy and appropriate tier distribution. The comparison of generation strategies (a regular one-shot pipeline versus a two-stage split pipeline for narrative OT books) provides practical guidance for similar systems. The deployed application at BibleGo.org, serving approximately 100--300 unique users per month with monthly page-view volumes ranging from ${\sim}200$ to ${\sim}2{,}500$ clicks, validates the real-world utility of the approach.

\paragraph{Future work.}
Several directions remain for future development:
\begin{itemize}[nosep,leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Unified OT delivery}: Consolidating the regular and split-pipeline OT corpora into a single served variant per verse, enabling cleaner head-to-head strategy evaluation on shared book sets.
    \item \textbf{Additional commentators}: Incorporating more modern and diverse theological perspectives into the corpus.
    \item \textbf{User studies}: Conducting formal user studies to evaluate BHT quality, usefulness, and theological accuracy as perceived by Bible study practitioners.
    \item \textbf{Enhanced original-language tools}: Expanding the word study features to include Hebrew morphology, cross-references, and parallel passage analysis.
    \item \textbf{Multilingual support}: Generating BHTs in languages beyond English to serve the global Bible study community.
    \item \textbf{Fine-tuned models}: Training domain-specific models on biblical commentary to reduce dependency on general-purpose LLMs and potentially improve both fidelity and cost efficiency.
\end{itemize}

By demonstrating that LLMs can be effectively constrained, scored, and validated to produce source-adherent scholarly summaries, BibleGoAI contributes both a practical tool for Bible study and a methodological framework applicable to other domains where multi-source synthesis with provenance tracking is required.
