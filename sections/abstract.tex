\textbf{BibleGoAI} is a deployed system that automatically synthesizes multi-author biblical commentary into concise, source-adherent summary paragraphs---called \textit{Brief Helpful Texts} (BHTs)---from a curated corpus of fourteen commentators spanning four centuries. Each BHT distills the most striking insights from a tiered corpus into a single 30--80 word paragraph, with automated quality scoring and provenance tracking via footnotes linking every claim back to its source commentary. The system combines large language models (LLMs), retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and semantic similarity scoring across a multi-stage pipeline: quote extraction, tiered synthesis, constraint-enforced generation with best-of-five selection, and cross-encoder footnote linking. We evaluate the system across 7{,}957 New Testament verses and the full 39-book Old Testament corpus, comparing a single-pass regular pipeline against a two-stage split pipeline for OT books. The resulting BHTs are served through BibleGo.org---a deployed web application providing interactive commentary exploration, original-language word study, and full commentator browsing---reaching approximately 100--300 unique users per month. Beyond the engineering contribution, BibleGoAI is also a record of a volunteer-driven faith-community collaboration: commentator curation by ministry leaders, a 28-volunteer digitization effort of Henry Alford's 2{,}544-page New Testament commentary, and iterative usability testing with the project's Bible study community.
