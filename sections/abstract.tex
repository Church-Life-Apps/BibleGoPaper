Centuries of biblical scholarship have produced a rich corpus of verse-by-verse commentary from theologians spanning diverse traditions and eras. However, accessing and synthesizing insights from multiple commentators remains a time-consuming task, even for dedicated students of the Bible. We present \textbf{BibleGoAI}, a system that leverages large language models (LLMs), retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and semantic similarity scoring to automatically produce concise, source-adherent summary paragraphs---called \textit{Brief Helpful Texts} (BHTs)---from curated multi-author commentary. Each BHT distills the most striking insights from a tiered corpus of up to fourteen commentators into a single 30--80 word paragraph, with automated quality scoring and provenance tracking via footnotes. We describe the full pipeline: quote extraction, tiered synthesis, retrieval-augmented generation for long-form commentary, iterative generation with constraint enforcement, and a composite scoring framework combining semantic accuracy and source fidelity. We evaluate the system across 7{,}957 New Testament verses and the full 39-book Old Testament corpus, comparing a single-pass regular pipeline against a two-stage split pipeline for OT books. The resulting BHTs are served through BibleGo.org---a deployed web application providing interactive commentary exploration, original-language word study, and commentator browsing---reaching approximately 100--300 unique users per month with monthly page-view volumes ranging from ${\sim}200$ to ${\sim}2{,}500$ clicks.
