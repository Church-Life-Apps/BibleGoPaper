Centuries of biblical scholarship have produced a rich corpus of verse-by-verse commentary from theologians spanning diverse traditions and eras. However, accessing and synthesizing insights from multiple commentators remains a time-consuming task, even for dedicated students of the Bible. We present \textbf{BibleGoAI}, a system that leverages large language models (LLMs), retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and semantic similarity scoring to automatically produce concise, source-adherent summary paragraphs---called \textit{Brief Helpful Texts} (BHTs)---from curated multi-author commentary. Each BHT distills the most striking insights from up to nine primary commentators into a single 30--80 word paragraph, with automated quality scoring and provenance tracking via footnotes. We describe the full pipeline: quote extraction, tiered synthesis, multi-strategy retrieval, iterative generation with constraint enforcement, and a composite scoring framework combining semantic accuracy, readability, and source fidelity. We evaluate the system across 7{,}957 New Testament and 10{,}492 Old Testament verses, comparing multiple generation strategies and preliminary cross-model evaluation. The resulting BHTs are served to hundreds of monthly users through BibleGo.org, a deployed web application providing interactive commentary exploration, original-language word study, and commentator browsing.
